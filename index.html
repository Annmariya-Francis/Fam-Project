SINGLE VISION

Single vision lenses are eyeglass lenses that provide vision correction for a single focal distance, either for nearsightedness (myopia), farsightedness (hyperopia), or astigmatism. They feature the same prescription across the entire lens surface and are designed to address vision issues at either near or far distances, but not both simultaneously. Most people who wear glasses utilize single vision lenses, especially for everyday activities like driving or reading.

PROGRESSIVE LENS

Progressive lenses, also known as multifocal or no-line bifocals, are eyeglass lenses that provide a gradual change in lens power to correct vision at different distances, eliminating the need for separate glasses for distance, intermediate, and near vision. These lenses seamlessly transition from distance correction at the top to near correction at the bottom, with an intermediate zone in between.

BIFOCAL GLASSESS

Bifocal glasses are designed to correct vision at both long and short distances, using two different lens powers within a single frame. The upper portion of the lens typically corrects distance vision, while the lower portion focuses on near vision for activities like reading. This eliminates the need to switch between two pairs of glasses and is particularly useful for individuals experiencing presbyopia, an age-related condition where the eyes lose their ability to focus on close objects.

Polarised LENSES

Polarized lenses in sunglasses reduce glare by filtering out horizontally polarized light, which is the type of light that bounces off surfaces like water, snow, and roads. This filtering enhances clarity and reduces eye strain, making them ideal for activities like driving, fishing, and spending time outdoors. The lenses contain a special chemical film with vertically aligned molecules that block horizontal light waves, allowing only vertical light waves to pass through.
